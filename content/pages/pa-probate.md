## Probate Gives You Authority. It Does Not Give You Time.

In Pennsylvania, the Register of Wills handles probate of wills and grants letters to the personal representative. Those letters give the executor or administrator authority to act for the estate, but they do not magically solve the property problem.

The executor may still have to handle a house full of belongings, mortgage payments, taxes, utilities, insurance, lawn/code issues, repairs, family emotions, and deadlines.

That is where Legacy Defenders fits.

We are not a law firm, tax preparer, lender, realtor, appraiser, or licensed trade contractor. We are the care-led operations team that helps the executor organize the home, belongings, local providers, records, and next decisions while working with the right professionals.

## The PA Probate Timeline Families Often Underestimate

<div class="probate-timeline">
  <article>
    <span>Step 1</span>
    <h2>Get authority</h2>
    <p>The will is probated or an administrator is appointed through the Register of Wills. Until authority is clear, major property decisions may be blocked or risky.</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <span>Step 2</span>
    <h2>Notify heirs and beneficiaries</h2>
    <p>Official county guidance commonly points to notice and certification requirements within three months after letters are granted.</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <span>Step 3</span>
    <h2>Inventory the estate</h2>
    <p>The executor needs records of estate assets. For a home, that can include the real estate and personal property inside it.</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <span>Step 4</span>
    <h2>File and pay inheritance tax</h2>
    <p>PA inheritance tax returns are generally due within nine months after death. PA also allows a 5% discount for tax paid within three months.</p>
  </article>
  <article>
    <span>Step 5</span>
    <h2>Preserve and distribute value</h2>
    <p>The home has to be maintained, belongings handled, service providers coordinated, debts reviewed, records preserved, and sale/keep/rent/move-in decisions made.</p>
  </article>
</div>

Sources and official references:

- The PA Department of Revenue states inheritance tax rates are 0% for surviving spouse transfers, 4.5% for direct descendants and lineal heirs, 12% for siblings, and 15% for other heirs, with charitable organizations and certain exempt entities excluded. See [PA Department of Revenue: Inheritance Tax](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/revenue/resources/tax-types-and-information/inheritance-tax).
- PA's REV-1500 form states resident inheritance tax returns must be filed within nine months after death. See [PA REV-1500](https://www.pa.gov/content/dam/copapwp-pagov/en/revenue/documents/formsandpublications/formsforindividuals/inheritancetax/documents/rev-1500.pdf).
- The PA Department of Revenue says inheritance tax payments are due at death, delinquent after nine months, and eligible for a 5% discount if paid within three months. See [Make an Inheritance Tax Payment](https://www.pa.gov/services/revenue/make-an-inheritance-tax-payment).
- Philadelphia's Register of Wills resources include post-probate forms for notice, certification of notice, estate inventory, and status report. See [Philadelphia Register of Wills Resources](https://www.phila.gov/departments/register-of-wills/resources/).
- Chester County's official estate settlement packet describes notice, inventory, inheritance tax, and status-report timing. See [Chester County Estate Settlement Packet](https://www.chesco.org/DocumentCenter/View/5052/Estate-Settlement-Packet?bidId=).

## Records For PA Inheritance Tax And Estate Accounting

PA's resident inheritance tax return is REV-1500. The executor, attorney, CPA, or tax preparer decides what belongs on that return and how estate expenses, reimbursements, deductions, asset values, donations, and sale proceeds should be handled.

Legacy Defenders helps by keeping the house side organized so the executor has a clearer paper trail for professional review:

- utility, mortgage, insurance, tax, lawn, vacant-home, and maintenance notes
- repair scopes, invoices, receipts, and before/after photos
- QR inventory exports, item photos, sale logs, storage logs, shipping notes, donation/disposal records, and distribution notes
- provider contact records and project status reports
- financing scopes, draw/milestone notes, and questions for attorney/lender review

The point is not to replace professional advice. The point is to give the professionals a clearer record so the executor is not trying to reconstruct the house project from memory months later.

## When There Is Equity But No Cash

Many estates have value trapped in the house, but little available cash to pay the mortgage, utilities, taxes, insurance, lawn care, cleanout, repairs, storage, or market-ready preparation.

Legacy Defenders can help the executor prepare a custom stabilization and funding plan:

- identify urgent carrying costs and deadlines
- estimate the work needed to protect the home and unlock value
- separate must-do stabilization from optional improvements
- prepare scopes, photos, and budgets for attorney, CPA, family, lender, or sale-proceeds review
- identify saleable items, vehicles, equipment, or other estate assets that may help fund approved work when authority is clear
- coordinate payment structures only after the right authority and professional review are in place

The goal is to help the executor and heirs make the best informed decision available: sell, keep, rent, move in, refinance, repair in phases, or use a financing path designed to protect rather than exploit the estate.
