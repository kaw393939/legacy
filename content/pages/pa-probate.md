## Probate Gives Authority. It Does Not Manage The House.

In Pennsylvania, probate can give the executor or administrator authority to act for the estate. It does not secure the home, pay the utilities, sort belongings, choose providers, organize receipts, or decide whether the house should be sold, kept, repaired, rented, or prepared for market.

That is the gap Legacy Defenders helps with.

## Pennsylvania Timing To Know

Pennsylvania inheritance tax is generally connected to the date of death. The PA Department of Revenue says payments become delinquent after nine months and may qualify for a 5% discount if paid within three months. The executor should review timing, filing, deductions, reimbursements, and estate accounting with the appropriate attorney or tax professional.

The house can make those deadlines harder because the family is still dealing with belongings, bills, repairs, family decisions, and sale timing. That is why getting the property side organized early matters.
