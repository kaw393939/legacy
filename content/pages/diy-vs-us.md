## You May Be Able To Do Much Of This Yourself

Many families can handle parts of an inherited home without hiring a full service team. You may be able to secure the house, gather bills, sort belongings, call providers, and coordinate repairs.

The question is not “Can I do anything myself?” The better question is:

**“Can I do this fast enough, calmly enough, and with enough records to protect the estate?”**

## DIY May Work If

<div class="ld-path-grid">
  <article class="ld-path-card trusted">
    <span>Good DIY fit</span>
    <h3>You have time and local help</h3>
    <ul>
      <li>You can visit the house regularly.</li>
      <li>Authority is clear.</li>
      <li>The family is mostly aligned.</li>
      <li>The home is safe enough to enter.</li>
      <li>You already have trusted cleaners, movers, storage, and contractors.</li>
      <li>You can track receipts, photos, item decisions, and provider work.</li>
    </ul>
  </article>

  <article class="ld-path-card warning">
    <span>Get help sooner</span>
    <h3>The house is becoming a second job</h3>
    <ul>
      <li>You are out of town.</li>
      <li>The house is full of belongings.</li>
      <li>Bills, utilities, insurance, or lawn/code issues are urgent.</li>
      <li>You do not know which providers to trust.</li>
      <li>The estate has value but little available cash.</li>
      <li>You feel pushed to decide before the situation is clear.</li>
    </ul>
  </article>
</div>

## What Legacy Defenders Does Differently

We do not take the executor’s decision-making authority away. We organize the house-side work so the executor can make fewer decisions with better information.

| Executor Burden | How We Help |
|---|---|
| The home needs attention | Property walkthrough, urgent issue list, provider needs, photo notes |
| Belongings block everything | Inventory, item routing, storage, shipping, donation, disposal, sale-support records |
| Providers are hard to coordinate | Trusted local provider calls, scopes, scheduling, photos, invoices |
| Costs are unclear | Estate Math Snapshot, cost categories, funding questions, professional handoff notes |
| Records get scattered | Receipts, invoices, photos, item logs, provider notes, closeout packet |

## Start With The Smallest Useful Step

You do not have to decide today whether to renovate, sell, rent, keep, donate, ship items, store belongings, or finance the work.

The first useful step is usually:

1. Talk through what happened.
2. Identify what is urgent.
3. Decide whether the $250 Estate Math Home Visit would help.
4. Use the visit to create a practical next-step plan.
