## Start Small Before Spending Big

Most families do not need to approve a large project on the first call. They need to understand what is urgent, what can wait, what they can do themselves, and what the estate may need to budget for.

| Step | Example Cost | What You Get |
|---|---:|---|
| Free one-hour estate call | Free | A calm conversation about the house, authority, belongings, deadlines, and first concerns |
| Estate Math Home Visit | $250 | A walkthrough of the home, belongings, obvious risks, likely providers, and cost categories |
| Visit credit | $250 credit | Applied toward approved Legacy Defenders services for the same property when the project moves forward |
| Detailed written scope | $750-$2,500+ | A deeper project plan when quotes, family decisions, financing review, or provider coordination are needed |

## Clear Item Handling Fees

Belongings are often the bottleneck. The estate owns the items and sale proceeds belong to the estate after approved fees, marketplace costs, shipping, storage, or handling.

| Service | Example Pricing |
|---|---:|
| Basic item route plan | Included in $250 visit |
| QR inventory setup | $750-$1,750 |
| Care coordinator / inventory labor | $75-$125/hr |
| Small item prep | $10-$25/item or batch priced |
| Medium item prep | $35-$75/item |
| Large item prep | $75-$175/item |
| Specialty or high-value research | $150-$500+ |
| Packing or shipping coordination | Handling fee + pass-through costs |
| Executor records export | $250-$1,000 |

## What A First Project Might Look Like

An executor relief sprint might include:

- a credited $250 home visit
- room photos and priority item notes
- a belongings decision session
- storage, donation, disposal, shipping, or sale-support routing
- provider calls for cleaning, moving, lawn, maintenance, or repairs
- receipts, invoices, photos, and next-step records

An example first sprint may cost around **$8,500-$12,000**. The goal is not to solve every possible issue. The goal is to clear the first bottleneck and give the executor a documented next decision.

## What We Do Not Decide For You

Legacy Defenders is not a law firm, CPA firm, lender, realtor, appraiser, insurance agency, or licensed trade contractor. We help organize the house-side work so the executor and appropriate professionals have clearer information.
