Pricing Philosophy

Families should not have to guess what help costs while they are grieving. Legacy Defenders uses example ranges, written scopes, item tracking, and phase-based approval so the executor can make informed decisions.

Our goal is not to make money because the estate is confused. Our goal is to make money by helping the estate do better: fewer panic decisions, faster access to trusted vendors, better documentation, better vendor coordination, more useful belongings decisions, and a clearer path to sell, keep, rent, or move into the home.

Pricing below is for planning only. Every home, estate, family, property condition, legal situation, storage need, and repair scope is different.

Aligned with the estate: We do not need to buy the house at a discount to create value. We get paid for care coordination, inventory, moving, storage routing, item processing, repairs, documentation, vendor coordination, and project management. When appropriate, we also help identify estate assets that may offset approved costs.

How Item Sales Work

We prefer a transparent fee model instead of taking a hidden percentage of the family story.

  • The estate owns the items.
  • Approved sale items can be moved to central sale storage so the house can be cleaned, repaired, shown, rented, sold, or used without turning the home into a public estate sale.
  • AI can help identify items and organize research, but family decisions and final sale strategy are reviewed with a care coordinator.
  • We use real marketplace sold-comps and time-to-sell signals where available so expectations are grounded.
  • When appropriate, faster-converting assets such as vehicles, equipment, or clearly saleable personal property may be reviewed as possible ways to fund urgent estate needs.
  • Sale proceeds belong to the estate after approved fees, marketplace costs, shipping, or handling.
  • We can charge for cleaning, photos, listing prep, storage, delivery, and records.
  • Items are reviewed at 30/60/90 days so storage does not quietly become the problem.
  • If an item is not worth selling, donation, return, bundle sale, or disposal may be the better decision.

How Property Distribution Works

Belongings can carry legal, financial, and emotional meaning. We help document and route property according to the will, executor authority, and approved family instructions.

  • Items can be color-tagged as keep, sell, donate, discard, store, deliver, return to house, or research/appraise.
  • Each handled item can have a photo, route, location, status, and notes.
  • Items kept by the family can go to client-controlled storage, where the executor can have key/access options.
  • Items approved for sale can go to central sale storage for cleaning, photos, listing, and review.
  • Items going to heirs can be scheduled for pickup, delivery, or return to the home.
  • Records can be exported for the executor, attorney, CPA, tax preparer, or family file.

Legacy Defenders does not decide who legally receives property. We help execute and document the approved instructions once authority and family/legal direction are clear.

Preferred Professional Network

You can use your own attorney, CPA, tax preparer, realtor, lender, or licensed trade contractor. If you need introductions, we can connect you with preferred or affiliated professionals who understand inherited homes and our standardized records.

Because our process produces organized photos, inventories, receipts, scopes, storage logs, sale records, and decision history, partner attorneys and CPAs may be able to offer more efficient packages or discounted rates where available.

Legal and tax advice comes from those professionals directly. Their fees, engagement terms, and any discounts should be disclosed before you approve the work.

Financing Options

Some estates have home equity but no available cash. In those situations, we can help prepare a scope and documentation package for attorney and lender review.

Possible paths may include:

  • sale of approved estate assets, such as vehicles or saleable personal property
  • estate funds
  • direct payment
  • monthly payments where appropriate
  • payment from sale proceeds where legally approved
  • third-party estate improvement or hard-money style financing
  • milestone-based draws for larger projects
  • carrying-cost support for mortgage, utilities, taxes, insurance, maintenance, or urgent property needs when reviewed and approved by the right professionals

Any financing, lien, repayment agreement, or sale-proceeds arrangement should be reviewed by the executor's attorney and the appropriate lender. The point of financing should be to preserve estate value, not trap the family.

For prearranged families, financing conversations can happen before the crisis. The family can discuss preferred funding sources, likely project sizes, insurance and maintenance concerns, and whether future sale proceeds, estate funds, direct payment, or third-party financing should be reviewed with professionals.

Why This Can Feel Like It Pays For Itself

We cannot promise a project will be free. What we can do is design the work around value:

  • avoid panic-sale discounts
  • move faster through trusted vendor relationships
  • recover value from saleable items
  • review vehicles or other faster-converting estate assets when appropriate
  • document donation/disposal decisions
  • reduce repeated handling of belongings
  • centralize vendor scopes, receipts, photos, and updates
  • focus repairs on what improves the next decision
  • keep the executor informed before costs grow

The right question is not, "What does cleanout cost?" The better question is:

What does the estate lose if the family is forced to sell too fast because no one can solve the whole problem?
