Pricing Philosophy

Families should not have to guess what help costs while they are grieving. Many heirs have never managed a house, hired contractors, coordinated cleanout, priced storage, handled shipping, or built an estate record file. Then suddenly all of it is urgent.

Legacy Defenders starts with the estate math: what needs to happen, what it may cost, what can be done yourself, which local services are needed, and what work should wait until the executor, attorney, CPA, realtor, lender, or family has better information.

Our goal is not to make money because the estate is confused. Our goal is to make money by helping the estate do better: faster access to trusted local services, better documentation, clearer belongings decisions, fewer repeated provider calls, less panic pricing, and a better path to sell, keep, rent, move in, finance, or pause with the house protected.

Pricing below is for planning only. Every home, estate, family, property condition, legal situation, storage need, shipping need, item value, and repair scope is different.

Aligned with the estate: We do not need to buy the house at a discount or take a hidden percentage of item sales to create value. We get paid for estate strategy, coordination, inventory, item handling, moving, storage routing, shipping support, repairs, documentation, provider coordination, and project management.

How Item Sales Work

We prefer a transparent fee model instead of taking a hidden percentage of the family story.

  • The estate owns the items.
  • Sale proceeds belong to the estate after approved fees, marketplace costs, shipping, storage, or handling.
  • Legacy Defenders charges for cleaning, photos, listing prep, packing, shipping coordination, storage coordination, delivery, records, and project management.
  • Approved sale items can move to central sale storage so the house can be cleaned, repaired, shown, rented, sold, or used without turning the home into a public bargain-hunter estate sale.
  • AI can help identify items and organize research, but family decisions and final sale strategy are reviewed with a care coordinator.
  • We use real marketplace sold-comps and time-to-sell signals where available so expectations are grounded.
  • Items are reviewed at 30/60/90 days so storage does not quietly become the problem.
  • If an item is not worth selling, donation, return, bundle sale, shipping to family, or disposal may be the better decision.

How Property Distribution Works

Belongings can carry legal, financial, and emotional meaning. We help document and route property according to the will, executor authority, and approved family instructions.

  • Items can be color-tagged as keep, sell, donate, discard, store, deliver, ship, return to house, or research/appraise.
  • Each handled item can have a photo, route, location, status, and notes.
  • Items kept by the family can go to client-controlled storage, where the executor can have key/access options.
  • Items approved for sale can go to central sale storage for cleaning, photos, listing, and review.
  • Items going to heirs can be scheduled for pickup, delivery, shipping, or return to the home.
  • Records can be exported for the executor, attorney, CPA, tax preparer, or family file.

Legacy Defenders does not decide who legally receives property. We help execute and document the approved instructions once authority and family/legal direction are clear.

Financing And Asset-Offset Options

Some estates have home equity but no available cash. In those situations, we can help prepare a scope and documentation package for attorney and lender review.

Possible paths may include:

  • sale of approved estate assets, such as vehicles or saleable personal property
  • estate funds
  • direct payment
  • staged project phases
  • monthly payments where appropriate
  • payment from sale proceeds where legally approved
  • third-party estate improvement or hard-money style financing
  • milestone-based draws for larger projects
  • carrying-cost support for mortgage, utilities, taxes, insurance, maintenance, or urgent property needs when reviewed and approved by the right professionals

Any financing, lien, repayment agreement, or sale-proceeds arrangement should be reviewed by the executor's attorney and the appropriate lender. The point of financing should be to preserve estate value, not trap the family.

Why This Can Feel Like It Pays For Itself

We cannot promise a project will be free. What we can do is design the work around value:

  • avoid panic-sale discounts
  • reduce time spent calling and comparing providers
  • use preferred provider pricing where available
  • recover value from saleable items
  • review vehicles or other faster-converting estate assets when appropriate
  • document donation/disposal decisions
  • reduce repeated handling of belongings
  • centralize provider scopes, receipts, photos, and updates
  • focus repairs on what improves the next decision
  • keep the executor informed before costs grow

The right question is not, "What does cleanout cost?" The better question is:

What does the estate lose if the executor has to solve every provider, item, repair, record, and funding problem alone while grieving?
