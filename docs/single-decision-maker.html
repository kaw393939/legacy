Simpler authority still needs a real process

Being the only clear decision maker can help. It can reduce family meetings, repeated approvals, and confusion about who can say yes. But it does not remove the real work: property access, belongings, bills, repairs, records, provider calls, storage, shipping, and timing.

This situation is often the best fit for a fast, low-friction first step. We help you understand the estate math, choose the first bottleneck, and avoid spending money in the wrong order.

What to be careful about
  • Assuming one decision maker means the project is simple.
  • Starting cleanout before documents, photos, titles, sentimental items, and possible saleable assets are protected.
  • Paying providers before there is a written scope, budget, and record plan.
  • Losing receipts, invoices, and item decisions that the executor may need later.
