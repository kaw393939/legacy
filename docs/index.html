Getting Started

Legacy Defenders is for heirs, executors, and families who inherited a home and are overwhelmed by belongings, repairs, deadlines, mortgage or tax pressure, family decisions, distance, or grief.

The first step is a free one-hour estate call. We learn what happened, where the home is, who has authority, what is urgent, what is inside the house, and what decision the family is trying to make: sell, keep, rent, move in, finance, or prepare for probate next steps.

The $250 visit is a low-friction first paid step. We walk the home, review belongings, identify obvious risks, estimate cost categories, map provider needs, and create an Estate Math Snapshot. If you hire Legacy Defenders for approved services on that property, the $250 is credited toward the project under the terms confirmed before scheduling.

It is not legal or tax advice. It is a practical house-side plan: urgent issues, carrying-cost categories, belongings routes, likely local service providers, records to gather, possible funding paths, and next decisions to review with the executor, family, attorney, CPA, realtor, lender, or contractor.

No. Many families call because they do not know yet. Our job is to organize the facts, stabilize the property, document the options, and help you make informed decisions with more calm.

Yes. A Prearranged Legacy Readiness Plan lets a living parent or family document the home, belongings, wishes, preferred contacts, likely services, and activation plan before the crisis. This can give heirs a clearer path and may qualify the family for priority activation or a planning discount/service credit.

Belongings & Inventory

We use a care-led inventory process with photos and color-coded QR smart tags. Items can be marked to keep, sell, donate, discard, deliver to an heir, store, return to the house, or research/appraise. Each handled item can have a photo, location, status, and notes so the executor can track what is happening.

Yes, when authority and instructions are clear. We do not decide who legally receives property, but we can help the executor route items according to the will, attorney guidance, executor instructions, and approved family decisions. We can document pickup, delivery, return-to-house, storage, sale, donation, or disposal status.

Items the family wants to keep can go to client-controlled storage, where the executor can have key or access options. Items approved for sale can go to central sale storage where they can be cleaned, photographed, listed, reviewed, and tracked without turning the family home into a public estate sale.

Our preferred model is to avoid turning the family home into a bargain-hunter event. Saleable items can be moved to a controlled processing/storage workflow where they can be cleaned, photographed, listed, tracked, and reviewed.

Our preferred model is not to take a hidden percentage of item sales. Sale proceeds belong to the estate after approved fees, marketplace costs, shipping, storage, or handling. Legacy Defenders charges for real work such as inventory, cleaning, photos, prep, packing, shipping coordination, storage coordination, listing support, records, and project management.

Item work can be priced by small, medium, large, specialty, batch, or hourly handling depending on the item and scope. Shipping, insurance, freight, boxes, supplies, storage-unit charges, marketplace fees, and carrier fees are usually pass-through costs. Handling, packing, coordination, photos, records, and delivery time are billed by the approved scope.

We use review points, usually around 30, 60, and 90 days, so the executor can decide whether to lower price, bundle, donate, retrieve, continue storage, or dispose of items. Storage should be an informed choice, not an endless hidden cost.
