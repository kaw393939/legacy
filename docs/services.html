Getting Started

Legacy Defenders is for heirs, executors, and families who inherited a home and are overwhelmed by belongings, repairs, deadlines, mortgage or tax pressure, family decisions, distance, or grief.

The first step is a care plan. We learn what happened, where the home is, who has authority, what is urgent, what is inside the house, and what decision the family is trying to make: sell, keep, rent, move in, or prepare for probate next steps.

Belongings & Inventory

We use a care-led inventory process with photos and color-coded QR smart tags. Items can be marked to keep, sell, donate, discard, deliver to an heir, store, return to the house, or research/appraise. Each handled item can have a photo, location, status, and notes so the executor can track what is happening.

Yes, when authority and instructions are clear. We do not decide who legally receives property, but we can help the executor route items according to the will, attorney guidance, executor instructions, and approved family decisions. We can document pickup, delivery, return-to-house, storage, sale, donation, or disposal status.

Items the family wants to keep can go to client-controlled storage, where the executor can have key or access options. Items approved for sale can go to central sale storage where they can be cleaned, photographed, listed, reviewed, and tracked without turning the family home into a public estate sale.

Our preferred model is to avoid turning the family home into a bargain-hunter event. Saleable items can be moved to a controlled processing/storage workflow where they can be cleaned, photographed, listed, tracked, and reviewed.

We use review points, usually around 30, 60, and 90 days, so the executor can decide whether to lower price, bundle, donate, retrieve, continue storage, or dispose of items. Storage should be an informed choice, not an endless hidden cost.
